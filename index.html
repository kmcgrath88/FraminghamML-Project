Framingham Heart Study


A long-term prospective study of the etiology of cardiovascular disease among a population of subjects
in the community of Framingham, MA beginning in 1948.


About the Study


This was the first prospective study of cardiovascular disease and identified the concept of risk factors and their joint effects. The study began in 1948, with 5,209 subjects initially enrolled. Each subject was examined biennially and were regularly surveilled for cardiovascular disease outcomes. For further information about the study please visit: https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/science/framingham-heart-study-fhs Key findings: the importance of high blood pressure and high cholesterol are risk factors for cardiovascular disease.

Analysis


This data is a subset of the entire study data set obtained from the NHBLI as a teaching data set (https://biolincc.nhlbi.nih.gov/teaching/). There were 4,434 participants included in this data set, with clinical data obtained from 1-3 examinations from 1956-1968. Each participant was followed for a total of 24 years. Outcomes of the following events were recorded: Angina Pectoris, Myocardial Infarction, Stroke, Death Risk factors recorded: sex, age, systolic blood pressure, diastolic blood pressure, use of anti-hypertensive medications, current cigarette smoking at exam, number of cigarettes smoked per day, serum total cholesterol, BMI, casual serum glucose, diabetes, heart rate, and prevalent cardiovascular disease conditions at time of exam.
